Inflation Reduction Act

Overview

The Inflation Reduction Act is a new law that helps Asian American, Native Hawaiian, and Pacific Islander communities by providing targeted relief from rising prices and increasing economic opportunity. The Act includes a number of provisions to help these communities, including:

  • Providing targeted relief from rising prices for low-income families and individuals.
  • Increasing economic opportunity for small businesses and entrepreneurs.
  • Providing funding for community-based organizations that serve AANHPI communities.

Provisions

Targeted Relief from Rising Prices

The Act includes a number of provisions to provide targeted relief from rising prices for low-income families and individuals, including:

  • Expanding the Earned Income Tax Credit to provide additional relief to low-income working families and individuals.
  • Providing additional funding for the Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP) to help low-income families afford food.
  • Providing additional funding for the Low Income Home Energy Assistance Program (LIHEAP) to help low-income families afford home energy costs.

Increasing Economic Opportunity

The Act includes a number of provisions to increase economic opportunity for small businesses and entrepreneurs, including:

  • Providing additional funding for the Small Business Administration (SBA) to help small businesses access capital and technical assistance.
  • Providing additional funding for community development financial institutions (CDFIs) to increase access to capital
  • Providing additional funding for the Minority Business Development Agency (MBDA) to help minority-owned businesses access capital and technical assistance.
  • Providing additional funding for the Office of Federal Procurement Policy (OFPP) to increase opportunities for small businesses, including those owned by minorities and women, to participate in federal procurement.

Support for Community-Based Organizations

The Act includes a number of provisions to provide funding for community-based organizations that serve AANHPI communities, including:

  • Providing additional funding for the Office of Minority Health (OMH) to support community-based organizations that promote health equity for AANHPI communities.
  • Providing additional funding for the Office of Community Services (OCS) to support community-based organizations that provide services to low-income families and individuals.
